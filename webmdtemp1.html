Our Challange

WebMD is well known for being a key source of health issues and having a vast amount of content available to its users. However, given competition in the online health space is growing rapidly, the management believes that users want a more personalized experience leading to frequent (and maybe even daily) engagement and better retention.

My Role

My most prominent roles were in: ideation, concept creation, wearable proptotyping, survey and interview question development, interviews, design, task analysis, user testing, presentation creation and delivery.

Initial research findings

Lot's of people go to WebMD (becasue of SEO), but they go infrequently and don't stay long

People get lost in the sea of health information, often scary, ad-filled, and not pertinent to them or their family

Our research validated WebMD's concerns that people need a more personalized experience

People are comfortable using apps for health information and are also very interested in trying wearables

WebMD needs to implement a really innovative idea to take a ~4x per year visitor to someone that uses WebMD as part of a daily habit

Checking in with Business Goals

Must. Have. Ads. Lots. Of. Ads.

Many of our competitors were reluctant to recommend changes to WebMd website becasue of its ads. Ads are the main revenue stream for the site (although not for the company overall) and the pervasive business concern.
But we think they can't see the forest for the trees.

With our approach people would be much more likely to come to WebMD and stay longer. Therefore, reducing the ads on the app, even drastically, won't mean reduced ad visibility, it will actually be much higher.

Design decisions

1

P

Allow them to accept, reject, sort, and re-filter their lists until they are satisfied that they have gotten what they need as well as their money's worth.

- Create a new target search page with better options

- Created an ability to archive lists so that salespeople could review prior searches and be assure they always received fresh new leads

2

Personmalize news feed, significantly reduced clutter

Tier5 is run by Millennials and many of their client come to them for twitter, Klout, and other offerings that help in marketing to Millennials so changing the look, logo and attitude of the UI to match the fresh young organization made good sense.

- Recreated landing page to offer a new, fresh, fun perspective and what the salesperson really wants - to work less.

- Completely redesigned/recreated the company logo

3

The ever-moving NAV.

We tested 5+ different versions of the NAV and the list components, but finally found a combination that most people found to be clear and easy to use.
